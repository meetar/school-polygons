Does Your School Have a Polygon?

Simple visualization of OpenStreetMap schools that don't have polygons to encourage edits.

For schools, polygons are twice as nice!

Welcome to our Targeted Editing series where today you can eat a peppermint, or make OpenStreetMap data excellent! (both take about the same amount of time)

  1. How can school polygons be used?

    1. Help with map rendering! No, this doesn't mean cook the fat out of it, this means help with how things are cartographically displayed on the map. A map label is great to have, but a polygon is even better!

    1. Here is a map styled by Peter Richardson & Nathaniel V. Kelso showing schools that don't have polygons.

      2. Peter's Tangram map will go right here and it will be awwwwwwwwwesome!

    2. To add a polygon for a school that is only represented by a point, click one of the red school points to bring up an info bubble with links to editors that can be used to add features!

        3. See the wiki pages for education features and more specifically amenity=school for lots of details and an great example.

    3. Not familiar with Atlanta? Search for your home town or one near it and pan over to an area you know.
